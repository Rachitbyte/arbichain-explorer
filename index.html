The Problem With Ethereum Mainnet

⚡

Congestion

Ethereum processes ~15 transactions per second. During peak demand, this leads to network congestion and failed transactions.

💸

High Gas Fees

A simple token transfer on Ethereum mainnet can cost $5–$50 in gas fees during busy periods, making small transactions economically unviable.

🔒

But Ethereum's Security Is Non-Negotiable

Ethereum's decentralization and security are its greatest assets. The solution wasn't to replace Ethereum — it was to build on top of it.

Enter Arbitrum: Ethereum's Leading Layer 2

🚀

Optimistic Rollups

Arbitrum bundles hundreds of transactions into a single batch and posts it to Ethereum. Transactions are assumed valid unless challenged — this is called Optimistic Rollup technology.

⚙️

EVM Compatible

Every smart contract, tool, and wallet that works on Ethereum works on Arbitrum without any changes. Developers deploy the exact same Solidity code.

🔗

Inherited Security

Arbitrum doesn't run its own consensus. All transaction data is posted to Ethereum mainnet, meaning Arbitrum inherits Ethereum's battle-tested security.
